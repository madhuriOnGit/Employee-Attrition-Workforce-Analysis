Employee Attrition & Workforce Analysis
Overview

The Employee Attrition & Workforce Analysis project aims to analyze employee data to identify key factors contributing to workforce attrition and provide actionable insights for improving employee retention. Using data analysis and visualization techniques, this project explores employee demographics, job roles, compensation, satisfaction levels, and other workplace factors that influence attrition.

Objectives
Analyze employee attrition trends.
Identify factors affecting employee turnover.
Understand workforce demographics and distribution.
Explore relationships between employee satisfaction, compensation, and attrition.
Generate insights to support HR decision-making and retention strategies.

Key Findings
Employees with lower job satisfaction showed higher attrition rates.
Certain departments experienced significantly higher turnover.
Employees with fewer years at the company were more likely to leave.
Work-life balance and compensation played important roles in employee retention.
Attrition varied across different job roles and age groups.
